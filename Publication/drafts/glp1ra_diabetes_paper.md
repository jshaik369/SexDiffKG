# Sex-Specific Safety Profiles of Diabetes Medications: 
# A Comprehensive Pharmacovigilance Analysis Reveals GLP-1 Receptor Agonists
# as Male-Biased for Adverse Events

Mohammed Javeed Akhtar Abbas Shaik (J.Shaik)
CoEvolve Network, Independent Researcher, Barcelona, Spain
jshaik@coevolvenetwork.com | ORCID: 0009-0002-1748-7516

## Abstract

**Background:** GLP-1 receptor agonists (semaglutide, liraglutide) have become among the most 
prescribed drugs worldwide. Sex differences in their safety profiles remain poorly characterized.

**Methods:** Analysis of 96,281 sex-differential signals from 14.5 million FAERS reports across 
7 diabetes drug classes and 10 cardiovascular drug classes.

**Results:** GLP-1 receptor agonists showed the strongest male bias (24.6% female, 517 signals) 
among all diabetes drug classes. This contrasts sharply with thiazolidinediones (91.7% female, 108 
signals), representing a 67.1 percentage-point range within diabetes drugs. The full ranking:

| Drug Class | % Female | Signals |
|-----------|----------|---------|
| Thiazolidinediones | 91.7% | 108 |
| Sulfonylureas | 73.6% | 148 |
| Metformin | 62.4% | 399 |
| SGLT2 inhibitors | 59.7% | 320 |
| DPP-4 inhibitors | 57.7% | 248 |
| Insulin | 39.5% | 339 |
| GLP-1RA | 24.6% | 517 |

**Cardiovascular drugs showed similar within-class variation:** antiplatelets 82.4% female vs 
calcium channel blockers 47.5% female. ACE inhibitors (72.4%F) showed significantly more female 
bias than ARBs (49.4%F) despite similar indications.

**Interpretation:** The male-biased safety profile of GLP-1RAs has important implications for 
the ongoing weight-loss drug revolution. As semaglutide use expands to predominantly female 
populations for weight management, sex-specific safety monitoring should be prioritized.
