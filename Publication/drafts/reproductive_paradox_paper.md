## Abstract

**Background:** Sex differences in adverse drug reactions are well-documented, yet the relationship 
between a drug's target patient population and its adverse event sex profile remains unexplored.

**Methods:** We analyzed 96,281 sex-differential pharmacovigilance signals from 14.5 million FAERS 
reports (2004-2025) across 2,178 drugs. We compared adverse event sex profiles of drugs indicated 
primarily for female conditions (oral contraceptives, HRT, aromatase inhibitors, SERMs) versus 
male conditions (testosterone, antiandrogens).

**Results:** Drugs indicated for female conditions generated predominantly male-biased adverse event 
signals (11.0% female across 73 signals), while drugs indicated for male conditions generated 
predominantly female-biased signals (74.7% female across 83 signals). Specific examples: HRT 
showed 0% female signals (all male), SERMs 8.3% female, aromatase inhibitors 19.4% female. 
Conversely, testosterone showed 74.5% female and antiandrogens 75.0% female. This pattern — which 
we term the "Reproductive Paradox" — held across all drug categories analyzed.

**The paradox extends beyond reproductive drugs:** Anti-HER2 agents (used primarily in female breast 
cancer) showed only 20.9% female signals, CDK inhibitors (breast cancer) 38.3% female, while 
vaccines (sex-neutral) showed 42.1% female overall, and corticosteroids showed 69.3% female.

**Interpretation:** The complete inversion of expected sex bias suggests that drug safety signals 
reflect biological sex differences in drug metabolism and adverse event susceptibility rather than 
simply reflecting the sex distribution of drug users. When a drug is used predominantly by one sex, 
the reporting denominator is large enough to make minor sex effects statistically significant, but 
the OPPOSITE sex shows systematically stronger disproportionality signals. This has profound 
implications for pharmacovigilance methodology and sex-specific prescribing guidelines.

**Key Findings:**
- Female-indication drugs: 11.0% female AE signals (89% male)
- Male-indication drugs: 74.7% female AE signals
- HRT: 0% female (ALL signals male-biased)
- Testosterone: 74.5% female (most signals female-biased)
- Anti-HER2 (breast cancer): 20.9% female
- CDK inhibitors (breast cancer): 38.3% female
- GLP-1RA (weight-neutral): 24.6% female
- Thiazolidinediones: 91.7% female
- Vaccines: 42.1% female overall

## Methods

### Data Source
FAERS database, 87 quarters (2004Q1-2025Q3), 14,536,008 deduplicated reports 
(F: 8,744,397 / M: 5,791,611). Drug name normalization via DiAna dictionary 
(846,917 mappings, 53.9% resolution).

### Signal Detection
Sex-stratified ROR with minimum report thresholds. Log-ratio analysis with 
Bayesian shrinkage. 96,281 total sex-differential signals across 2,178 drugs.

### Drug Classification
- Female-indication: OCs, HRT, aromatase inhibitors, SERMs
- Male-indication: Testosterone, antiandrogens
- Sex-neutral controls: Vaccines, antibiotics, statins
