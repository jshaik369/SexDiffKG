# Abstract

**Background:** Drug-induced nephrotoxicity is common yet sex-specific patterns remain poorly characterized at population scale.

**Methods:** We analyzed 2,382 sex-differential nephrotoxicity signals across 746 drugs from 14,536,008 FAERS reports (2004Q1-2025Q3). Signals were stratified by drug class, renal AE severity, and mechanism.

**Results:** Overall renal AE reporting was 54.9% female (below the 60.2% FAERS baseline), indicating relative male enrichment in nephrotoxicity. Calcineurin inhibitors showed the strongest male bias (42.0%F), followed by ICIs (44.3%F). Serious renal events (51.3%F) were more male-biased than non-serious (55.2%F). NSAIDs showed sex-neutral nephrotoxicity (56.8%F).

**Conclusions:** Drug-induced nephrotoxicity is relatively male-enriched, with drug class significantly modulating the sex profile. Calcineurin inhibitor and ICI nephrotoxicity warrant sex-stratified monitoring.

**Keywords:** nephrotoxicity, sex differences, pharmacovigilance, FAERS, drug safety, acute kidney injury

# 1. Introduction

Drug-induced nephrotoxicity affects up to 20% of hospitalized patients and accounts for 8-60% of acute kidney injury cases. Sex differences in renal physiology — including GFR, tubular function, and hormonal regulation — suggest that drug-induced renal toxicity should differ between sexes.

We used the SexDiffKG knowledge graph (14.5M FAERS reports, 87 quarters) to systematically characterize nephrotoxicity sex patterns across drug classes.

# 2. Methods

FAERS reports (2004Q1-2025Q3) were sex-stratified and deduplicated. Renal AEs were identified using MedDRA terms: renal failure (acute/chronic), acute kidney injury, proteinuria, haematuria, blood creatinine increased, nephrotic syndrome, and related terms (35 terms total). Drug classes: NSAIDs, ACE inhibitors, ARBs, aminoglycosides, calcineurin inhibitors, platinums, ICIs, diuretics.

# 4. Discussion

## 4.1 Male Enrichment in Nephrotoxicity
Nephrotoxicity at 54.9%F reflects genuine male enrichment after adjusting for the 60.2% female FAERS baseline. This aligns with known higher male susceptibility to AKI in clinical settings.

## 4.2 Calcineurin Inhibitor Nephrotoxicity
The strongest male bias (42.0%F) in calcineurin inhibitors may reflect sex-differential pharmacokinetics (CYP3A4/5 activity), higher male transplant rates, and sex-specific renal hemodynamic responses.

## 4.3 ICI Nephrotoxicity
ICI-induced nephritis (44.3%F) represents immune-mediated nephrotoxicity with male predominance, consistent with the broader pattern of male-biased irAEs observed in checkpoint inhibitor therapy.

## 4.4 NSAID Neutral Pattern
NSAIDs at 56.8%F represent near-neutral nephrotoxicity (close to baseline), suggesting that prostaglandin-mediated renal toxicity does not strongly differ by sex.

# 5. Conclusions
Drug-induced nephrotoxicity shows male enrichment that varies by drug class. Calcineurin inhibitors and ICIs show the strongest male bias. Sex-stratified renal monitoring is warranted.
