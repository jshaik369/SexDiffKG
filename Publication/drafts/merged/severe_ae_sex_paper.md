# Sex Disparities in Drug-Induced Life-Threatening Adverse Events:
# Comprehensive Analysis of 3,827 Fatal and Severe Safety Signals

Mohammed Javeed Akhtar Abbas Shaik (J.Shaik)
CoEvolve Network, Independent Researcher, Barcelona, Spain
jshaik@coevolvenetwork.com | ORCID: 0009-0002-1748-7516

## Abstract

**Background:** Whether life-threatening drug adverse events show sex differences has not been 
systematically quantified across the pharmacopeia.

**Methods:** We extracted and analyzed sex-differential signals for 20 categories of severe/fatal 
adverse events from 14.5 million FAERS reports.

**Results:** Drug-related death signals were 73.1% female (450 signals, 337 drugs). Among the 
most sex-skewed severe AEs:

| Severe AE | % Female | Signals |
|----------|----------|---------|
| Neuroleptic malignant syndrome | 88.6% | 44 |
| Cardiac arrest | 84.8% | 210 |
| DIC | 83.8% | 68 |
| Myocardial infarction | 82.2% | 258 |
| Stevens-Johnson syndrome | 81.7% | 71 |
| Renal failure | 80.7% | 290 |
| Status epilepticus | 79.5% | 44 |
| Rhabdomyolysis | 78.9% | 123 |
| Death | 73.1% | 450 |
| Respiratory failure | 71.1% | 280 |
| Agranulocytosis | 70.1% | 77 |

**Notable male-biased exceptions:**
| Severe AE | % Female | Signals |
|----------|----------|---------|
| Pulmonary embolism | 22.4% | 196 |
| Deep vein thrombosis | 31.6% | 114 |
| Anaphylaxis | 46.2% | 199 |

**Interpretation:** The systematic female predominance in most life-threatening drug AEs (14 of 
20 categories >50% female) coupled with male-biased venous thromboembolism suggests organ-specific 
sex differences in drug toxicity susceptibility.
