I am writing to submit our manuscript entitled "The Anti-Regression Phenomenon: Sex Bias in Adverse Drug Reaction Reporting Intensifies With Statistical Power" for consideration as an Original Research Article in Drug Safety.

This study presents a counter-intuitive finding that challenges a core assumption in pharmacovigilance methodology. Through systematic analysis of 96,281 sex-differential signals extracted from 14.5 million FAERS reports, we demonstrate that the female predominance in adverse drug reaction reporting does not attenuate with increasing sample size, as regression-to-the-mean would predict, but instead intensifies monotonically. We term this the anti-regression phenomenon: when drug-event pairs are stratified into volume deciles, female reporting bias strengthens with perfect monotonicity (Spearman rho = 1.0, p = 6.6e-64) from the lowest to the highest reporting volumes.

This finding has direct methodological consequences for pharmacovigilance. Standard approaches to signal detection and disproportionality analysis often treat apparent sex imbalances in low-volume data as likely noise, expecting convergence toward parity as evidence accumulates. Our results demonstrate the opposite: sex differences in drug safety are structural features of the pharmacological landscape that become more pronounced, not less, as data accrue. This implies that pharmacovigilance systems should not discount sex-stratified signals on the basis of expected regression, and that sex-specific analysis should be applied systematically regardless of reporting volume.

I believe Drug Safety is the most appropriate venue for this work. As the official journal of the International Society of Pharmacovigilance, your readership comprises precisely the methodologists, regulators, and pharmacovigilance scientists for whom these findings have the most immediate relevance. Additionally, Zucker and Prendergast's widely-cited call for sex-specific pharmacovigilance (eClinicalMedicine, 2019) identified precisely the analytical gap that this manuscript addresses.
